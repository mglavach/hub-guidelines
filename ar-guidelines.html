Reading is about so much more than earning AR points. Students read for fun, to challenge themselves, to relax, and to discover new ideas. AR is simply a tool to track and celebrate the reading that is already happening.

Every book a student picks up, whether it has an AR quiz or not, builds vocabulary, empathy, knowledge, and imagination. The goal is always a lifelong love of reading first. AR points are just one small way to celebrate that journey.

Encourage your child to read books that excite them, books that challenge them, and books that let them unwind. The points will follow.

About the Program

What Is Accelerated Reader?

Accelerated Reader (AR) is a reading motivation and practice program used at WCS Elementary. Students read books they choose, take short comprehension quizzes, and earn points toward their annual reading goal. AR celebrates how much students read. It never restricts what they can read.

Motivation & Celebration

AR turns reading into something to be proud of. Every quiz passed and every point earned is a milestone worth celebrating on the path to the annual goal.

Goal Setting

Students set and track annual AR point goals by grade, building accountability and the habit of seeing themselves as readers who finish books and reflect on what they've read.

Wide Reading First

AR quizzes are available for thousands of books. Students are always encouraged to read widely across genres, formats, and levels, and let AR track what they read.

The AR Honor Code

AR points are only meaningful when they represent real reading. At WCS, we hold every student to a standard of honesty and integrity. Taking an AR quiz is a chance to show what you truly learned from a book you read.

  • Only take a quiz for a book you have read from beginning to end. Finishing the whole book is what earns the quiz.
  • Do not take a quiz based on a movie, TV show, or summary. Even if the story is familiar, the quiz is about the specific words, details, and events in the book itself.
  • Do not look up answers online or ask others for help during the quiz. Work independently and let your score reflect your own honest reading and understanding.
  • Let your points be something to be truly proud of. A lower score on a book you really read is worth far more than a high score that isn't honest.
  • Your score helps your teacher help you. AR quiz results show teachers how a student is truly doing as a reader. Honest scores allow teachers to see where each student is and provide the right support, challenge, or differentiation they need to grow.

Reading Goals

Annual AR Point Goals by Grade

Goals are set annually and track cumulative points earned across the school year. Points are awarded based on book length and quiz score.

Grade Annual AR Goal Reading Focus Sustained Reading Time Quiz Location
PK3 & PK4 N/A Listening comprehension, print awareness, love of stories Daily read-alouds; picture book exploration N/A
K N/A Concepts of print, story structure, oral retell 5–10 minutes; weekly book checkout N/A
1st Enrichment as needed Foundational decoding, fluency, comprehension 10–15 minutes; wide reading across formats Quizzes may be taken at home
2nd 25 pts Independent reading growth, vocabulary, story elements 15–20 minutes; fiction and nonfiction regularly Quizzes may be taken at home or school
3rd 50 pts Reading stamina, text evidence, genre comparison 20–25 minutes; one "stretch" book per quarter Quizzes must be taken at school
4th 75 pts Deeper comprehension, theme, cross-text connections 25–30 minutes; history, biography, poetry included Quizzes must be taken at school
5th 100 pts Analytical thinking, argument, reading independence 30+ minutes; long-form novels each quarter Quizzes must be taken at school

Point goals are cumulative across the school year. Students can earn partial credit for any quiz score above 60%.

Taking a Quiz

How It Works: Step by Step

1

Read Your Book

Finish the book from cover to cover. The quiz checks real comprehension, so reading the whole book gives you the best chance of success.

2

Log In to AR

Visit the Renaissance portal and log in with your username and password (see the login section above).

3

Find Your Book

Search for the title of the book you just finished. Not every book has a quiz; if yours doesn't, ask Mrs. Glavach about alternatives.

4

Take the Quiz

Answer the multiple-choice comprehension questions. Quizzes are usually 5–10 questions. Work independently and do your best!

5

See Your Score

AR shows your score right away. A score of 70% or higher earns passing and partial points; 100% earns the full point value for that book.

6

Track Your Progress

Watch your points add up toward your annual goal. Celebrate every milestone; each point represents a book read and a story experienced!

Best Practices

Tips for AR Success

For Students

  • Take the quiz as soon as you finish a book; details are freshest right away
  • Read books you genuinely enjoy; excitement shows in quiz scores
  • Mix up genres
  • Track your points regularly and celebrate progress toward your goal
  • Ask your teachers or Mrs. Glavach for book recommendations that match your interests
  • Aim for a quiz score of 70% or higher for full credit

For Parents

  • Ask your child what they are reading and what happened in the story
  • Check AR point progress at home using the student login
  • Encourage daily reading; even 15 minutes adds up significantly over the year
  • Visit your local library to supplement school books
  • Contact Mrs. Glavach if your child is struggling to reach their goal
  • Celebrate milestones: 25%, 50%, 75%, and 100% of the annual goal!
