About Our Space

What Is The Hub?

The Hub is a central, flexible, and welcoming space designed for students in Preschool through 5th grade. It serves as an extension of the classroom, a place where literacy, curiosity, enrichment learning, and digital citizenship all come together.

Students don't just visit The Hub, they grow here, guided by dedicated educators and surrounded by resources curated to spark wonder and deepen understanding.

Our Framework

The H.U.B. Pathway

Everything students do in The Hub flows through three interconnected stages of growth, a pathway that shapes how they engage with information and become confident learners.

H
Harness

Gather & Focus

Students actively gather, focus, and direct curiosity toward meaningful learning, identifying sources, collecting relevant information, and applying it to real questions.

  • Notice: identify interesting ideas
  • Collect: choose reliable sources
  • Focus: decide what matters most
  • Apply: use knowledge to learn
U
Understand

Connect & Make Meaning

Students make sense of ideas by analyzing, synthesizing, and reflecting, moving from isolated facts to rich, connected understanding across texts and experiences.

  • Compare: spot similarities and differences
  • Analyze: examine how parts fit together
  • Synthesize: combine ideas into new insight
  • Reflect: connect to what you already know
B
Become

Grow & Contribute

Students become confident, thoughtful, Christ-centered learners, applying knowledge, collaborating with others, and contributing positively to their world.

  • Practice: use skills in real situations
  • Collaborate: work with and for others
  • Reflect: notice how you have grown
  • Apply: serve and create meaningfully

Measurement & Growth

Assessment

Why Assessment?

Assessment in The Hub is not about labeling students or restricting their reading and math work. It is about understanding each learner so we can serve them better. When we know where a student is, we can celebrate how far they have come and guide them toward what is next.

Our assessments measure growth over time, inform instruction, and help teachers have meaningful conversations with students and families. They work alongside, never instead of, wide reading, mathematical curiosity, and the joy of learning.

Assessments

MAP and STAR are adaptive assessments that adjust question difficulty in real time to measure student ability and growth. IXL is an adaptive learning platform that adjusts practice based on student responses but is used primarily for instruction and skill development rather than formal benchmarking.

How We Understand Student Learning & Growth

WCS uses a balanced system of assessments to understand student learning, guide instruction, and support both growth and enrichment. Each type of assessment serves a distinct purpose, from long-term growth to daily skill development.

1 · Growth Measurement

Big Picture

MAP Growth

MAP is an adaptive assessment that measures long-term academic growth in reading and math.

  • Administered 2–3 times per year
  • Adjusts difficulty based on student responses
  • Provides a growth score (RIT)
  • Shows progress over time and across grade levels

Purpose: Understand where a student is and how they are growing over time.

2 · Benchmark & Screening

Quick Snapshot

STAR Assessments

STAR is a short, adaptive assessment used to quickly measure reading and math skills.

  • Administered multiple times per year
  • Efficient, fast, and adaptive
  • Helps with placement and progress monitoring
  • Identifies students needing support or enrichment

Purpose: Provide a quick, reliable snapshot of current performance.

3 · Reading Fluency

Skill Development

Oral Reading Fluency (ORF)

ORF measures how accurately and fluently a student reads aloud.

  • Timed one-minute reading passage
  • Measures accuracy, rate, and fluency
  • Used frequently for progress monitoring
  • Especially important in early literacy development

Purpose: Track reading fluency growth and guide targeted reading support.

4 · Daily Learning & Practice

Instructional Assessment

IXL

IXL and classroom assessments measure daily understanding of taught skills.

  • Aligned to weekly or unit instruction
  • Provides immediate feedback
  • Helps identify reteaching or enrichment needs
  • Supports mastery of specific standards

Purpose: Check understanding of recent learning and guide next steps in instruction.

How It Works Together

The Hub assessment system works as a connected cycle:

Teach → Assess → Respond → Differentiate → Grow
  • MAP shows long-term growth
  • STAR provides quick benchmarks
  • ORF monitors reading fluency progress
  • Classroom assessments guide daily instruction

Together, these tools ensure every student is supported, challenged, and growing.

Intervention

Why, When & How Intervention

Intervention at The Hub is a targeted, responsive layer of support, not a label. When assessment data or teacher observation signals that a student needs more, we act quickly and intentionally. Each pathway is designed to strengthen specific skills in the least-intrusive, most effective way possible, preserving the student's identity as a capable, growing learner.
