About Our Space

What Is The Hub?

The Hub is a central, flexible, and welcoming space designed for students in Preschool through 5th grade. It serves as an extension of the classroom, a place where literacy, curiosity, enrichment learning, and digital citizenship all come together.

Students don't just visit The Hub, they grow here, guided by dedicated educators and surrounded by resources curated to spark wonder and deepen understanding.

  • Develop a love of reading in print and digital forms
  • Strengthen digital citizenship and responsible technology use
  • Engage in enrichment activities that extend classroom learning
  • Cultivate curiosity, inquiry, and critical thinking
  • Grow academically, socially, and spiritually alongside caring teachers

Our Framework

The H.U.B. Pathway

Everything students do in The Hub flows through three interconnected stages of growth, a pathway that shapes how they engage with information and become confident learners.

H
Harnessing

Gather & Focus

Students actively gather, focus, and direct curiosity toward meaningful learning, identifying sources, collecting relevant information, and applying it to real questions.

  • Notice: identify interesting ideas
  • Collect: choose reliable sources
  • Focus: decide what matters most
  • Apply: use knowledge to learn
U
Understanding

Connect & Make Meaning

Students make sense of ideas by analyzing, synthesizing, and reflecting, moving from isolated facts to rich, connected understanding across texts and experiences.

  • Compare: spot similarities and differences
  • Analyze: examine how parts fit together
  • Synthesize: combine ideas into new insight
  • Reflect: connect to what you already know
B
Becoming

Grow & Contribute

Students become confident, thoughtful, Christ-centered learners, applying knowledge, collaborating with others, and contributing positively to their world.

  • Practice: use skills in real situations
  • Collaborate: work with and for others
  • Reflect: notice how you have grown
  • Apply: serve and create meaningfully

Literacy

Reading Program

Our reading program is built on the belief that wide, joyful reading creates the strongest readers, aligned to Florida B.E.S.T. ELA Standards for every grade level.

Wide Reading

Students read within, above, and below their level, exploring fiction, nonfiction, poetry, biography, graphic novels, and more. Genre variety builds vocabulary and empathy.

Sustained Independent Reading

Daily sustained silent reading builds stamina. From 5–10 minutes in Kindergarten to 30+ minutes in 5th grade, students grow as focused, independent readers.

AR as Motivation

Accelerated Reader celebrates reading milestones and encourages goal-setting, a tool for motivation and accountability, never a restriction on book choice.

Grade Reading Focus Sustained Reading Goal AR Points (Quarterly)
PK3 & PK4 Listening comprehension, print awareness, love of stories Daily read-alouds; independent picture book exploration; share favorite books with the class N/A
K Concepts of print, story structure, oral retell 5–10 minutes sustained attention; weekly book checkout; share favorite books with classmates N/A
1st Foundational decoding, fluency, comprehension monitoring 10–15 minutes; wide reading across formats; share book picks with classmates N/A
2nd Independent reading growth, vocabulary, story elements 15–20 minutes; fiction and nonfiction regularly; peer book recommendations 25 pts
3rd Reading stamina, text evidence, genre comparison 20–25 minutes; 5+ genres annually; one "stretch" book per quarter; peer book recommendations 50 pts
4th Deeper comprehension, theme, cross-text connections 25–30 minutes; wide reading including history, biography, poetry; peer book recommendations 75 pts
5th Analytical thinking, argument, reading independence 30+ minutes; long-form novels each quarter; lead peer book recommendations 100 pts

Measurement & Growth

Reading Assessment

Why Reading Assessment?

Assessment in The Hub is not about labeling students or restricting their reading, it is about understanding each learner so we can serve them better. When we know where a student is, we can celebrate how far they have come and guide them toward what is next.

Our assessments measure growth over time, inform instruction, and help teachers have meaningful conversations with students and families. They work alongside, never instead of, wide reading, curiosity, and the joy of books.

Assessment Types

MAPS Grades K–5

Measures of Academic Progress

MAP Growth · NWEA

A computer-adaptive benchmark assessment that adjusts in real time to each student's responses, delivering a precise RIT score that measures exactly where a student is, and how much they have grown from one testing window to the next.

Testing Windows

Fall Winter Spring

What It Measures

  • Reading comprehension and vocabulary
  • Foundational skills (K–2)
  • Literature and informational text
  • Growth from window to window

How We Use Results

  • Inform instruction and small-group support
  • Set individual growth goals with students
  • Guide family conferences
  • Celebrate measurable growth
STAR Grades 2–4

STAR Reading

Renaissance STAR

A computer-adaptive assessment that provides a Scaled Score and grade equivalent, offering a quick, reliable snapshot of reading comprehension and vocabulary to supplement MAP data and inform targeted instruction.

What It Measures

  • Reading comprehension
  • Vocabulary in context
  • Instructional reading level
Fluency Grades 1–3

Oral Reading Fluency

ORF / CBM-R

A brief, teacher-administered reading passage assessment that measures words read correctly per minute (WCPM), tracking the development of accurate, expressive, and automatic decoding as a foundation for comprehension.

What It Measures

  • Words correct per minute (WCPM)
  • Accuracy and expression
  • Decoding automaticity

Assessment results are shared with classroom teachers and families to celebrate growth and guide next steps, never to define or limit a reader.
