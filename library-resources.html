Our Purpose

Library Mission

The Westminster Christian School Library exists to empower learners, inspire lives, and prepare hearts through literacy and a love for learning. Grounded in a Christ-centered foundation, our library fosters a nurturing environment where students can explore knowledge, develop critical thinking, and grow in their faith. We celebrate the power of literature as both a window, offering glimpses into diverse experiences and God's work in the world, and a mirror, reflecting each student's identity as a beloved child of God. Through high-quality resources, engaging programs, and stories that cultivate empathy and understanding, we encourage students to embrace compassionate Christianity, loving others as Christ loves us. With a commitment to academic excellence and spiritual growth, we equip students to become lifelong learners and faithful ambassadors for Jesus Christ.

Rules & Expectations

  • Respect for Others: maintain a quiet atmosphere for reading and study
  • Respect for Books and Materials: handle all resources with care
  • Christian Conduct: speak and act in a manner that reflects our core values
  • Food and Drinks: not allowed in the library to protect materials
  • Technology Use: devices may only be used for educational purposes and with permission

Borrowing Books

Checkout & Returns

Loan Period

Books may be borrowed for 2 weeks and can be renewed if no one else is waiting.

Checkout Limit

Students may check out up to 2 books at a time.

Overdue Books

A reminder will be sent for late books. Persistent overdue books may result in limited borrowing privileges.

Lost or Damaged Books

Families will be responsible for the cost of replacement for lost or damaged items.

Get Involved

Library Programs & Activities

From weekly storytimes to monthly book club meetings, our library offers enriching programs for every age and interest.

PK through K5

Storytime & Read-Alouds

Weekly read-aloud sessions for younger students exploring picture books, Bible stories, poetry, and nonfiction that spark wonder and build listening comprehension.

Voluntary

Nerd Herd Book Club

Monthly sessions with engaging discussions for different grade levels. Students connect over shared reads, share recommendations, and grow as thoughtful readers and conversationalists.

All Grades

Reading Challenges

Encouraging students to explore different reading goals, genre milestones, and volume targets that celebrate growth, curiosity, and a love for books.

TechTime Elective

Research & Literacy Workshops

Helping students develop strong research skills, digital literacy, and the ability to evaluate sources through a biblical lens, preparing them for independent inquiry.

Parent Partners

Volunteer Opportunities

Volunteers are a vital part of our library community. Whether you love books, enjoy working with children, or want to support our Christ-centered mission, there are many ways to get involved.

Library Organization & Operations

Shelve and organize books, assist with check-in, prepare new books for circulation, and create engaging displays that highlight Christian values and seasonal themes.

Inspiring a Love for Reading

Read aloud during storytime, help students select books that match their interests, and support our window and mirror initiative by connecting students with diverse, affirming stories.

Special Events & Programs

Help organize author visits, book fairs, and literacy events. Assist with reading challenges and prepare activities for special programs throughout the year.

Technology & Research Support

Guide students in using digital resources for research with discernment, assist with basic research skills, and support STEM and literacy initiatives through a biblical lens.

Administrative & Behind the Scenes

Prepare materials for lessons and programs, assist with inventory and book donations, and help develop reading lists that align with academic excellence and Christian character.

Be a Part of Our Mission

Volunteering in the WCS Library is an opportunity to serve with purpose, nurture young hearts and minds, and help students grow as lifelong learners and faithful ambassadors of Christ.
