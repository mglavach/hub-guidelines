Technology at The Hub

The Hub builds adaptive, future-ready technology skills that prepare students for a rapidly changing digital world, teaching them how to use digital tools responsibly while integrating faith-based discernment into their online interactions. Students learn to leverage technology as a tool for creativity, communication, collaboration, and problem-solving.

Our Digital Media Specialist guides students through thoughtful evaluation of information, responsible digital citizenship, and ethical decision-making, developing the skills and character needed to use technology wisely and purposefully in a complex world.

Both our Literacy and Technology roles work hand in hand, ensuring students are equipped with skills rooted in biblical truth, preparing them to be wise, compassionate, and faithful learners in both print and digital spaces.

Students Will Develop

Source evaluation and research skills
Safe and ethical internet use
Digital creativity and media production
Coding basics and STEM exploration
Christ-centered discernment online

What We Teach

Four Areas of Focus

Digital Literacy, Responsible Research & Digital Citizenship

  • Guiding students in evaluating sources through a biblical lens, distinguishing between truth and misinformation
  • Instructing on safe and ethical internet use, emphasizing Christian values in digital citizenship
  • Teaching database navigation, online research strategies, and multimedia literacy

For Students

  • Be kind and respectful in all online communication
  • Use technology only for its intended educational purpose
  • Never share personal information without teacher or parent permission
  • Think before you post: would you be proud if your teacher saw this?
  • Report anything that feels unsafe or unkind to a trusted adult
  • Evaluate what you read online before accepting it as true

For Families

  • Keep devices in shared spaces at home for younger students
  • Have regular conversations about what your child is doing online and explore digital resources together
  • Set reasonable and consistent screen time boundaries
  • Model healthy technology habits and discernment in your own use

Educational Technology Integration

  • Supporting teachers in incorporating digital tools, apps, and online resources into the curriculum
  • Introducing students to interactive learning platforms, coding basics, and STEM-related technology
  • Encouraging faith-based media creation through podcasts, videos, and digital presentations

Discernment & Biblical Worldview Online

  • Teaching students to approach media with a Christ-centered perspective and critical thinking
  • Encouraging ethical behavior online: kindness, respect, and integrity in communication
  • Helping students and families navigate screen time balance and healthy technology habits

Faith in the Digital Age

  • Seek Truth: Students learn to seek truth with discernment, distinguishing reliable information from misinformation through a biblical lens
  • Love Your Neighbor: Online interactions are an extension of our call to love others. Kindness, respect, and integrity define how we communicate in digital spaces
  • Create with Purpose: Technology is a tool for good. Students create content that reflects their values, tells meaningful stories, and serves their community

Schoolwide Technology Initiatives

  • Managing access to digital learning resources, e-books, and multimedia tools
  • Providing professional development for teachers on faith-based technology integration
  • Leading workshops on internet safety, cybersecurity, and Christian media engagement
